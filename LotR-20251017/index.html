Aid to Fornost


This is a scenario of Games Workshop's Middle Earth Strategy Battle Game, from the Rise of Angmar sourcebook.

Hobbit archers are trying to make their way to join the Arnorian army facing down the Witch-king of Angmar at Fornost. The Good side wins if 6 or more hobbits leave the board via the northern edge. Evil wins by preventing a Good victory.

(Click on any image to see an enlargement.)

The Forces


The Good forces are 12 hobbit archers, escorted by three Rangers of the North.

The Evil forces are a regular sprue of orcs (4 with shield, 4 with spear, 2 with bow, 2 with two-handed weapon) led by an Angmar Orc Captain.

Setup


The board is narrower than normal, being two feet wide. The area to left that is bereft of trees is off-limits.

Evil sets up along the middle of the board. The orcs start in sentry mode, so there's some chance that the hobbits can escape without ever being detected. Spreading out like this minimizes the chance of that happening.

Good then sets up near the bottom of the board.

Turn 1


Good always starts narrative scenarios with Priority.

After setup and the photos were taken, Good decides that deploying in a line maybe wasn't so smart. Grouping everyone to one side or the other would have been much better. So the Good figures jink right as they move forward.

Evil rolls for each model per the sentry rules. There are no 1s (halt in place), two 6s (move as normal), and the usual mix of 2,3 (Good half-moves the model) and 4-5 (Evil half-moves the model).

Post-game Thoughts

Ugh, that wasn't close at all.

I think a large part of the problem is that I didn't have enough trees on the board. I tried to more or less recreate the scenario map, but that didn't provide enough cover for the hobbits to do much sneaking about.

I generally like scenarios using the sentry rules, as they add some tension as the sentries close in, and hilarity as they then walk in the wrong direction. But this playthrough just didn't work. The hobbits are so much weaker than orcs that in any straight-up match they're going to lose, and the alarm raising so early meant that a straight-up match is exactly what they faced.

Based on this experience, I'd give the scenario two stars. But I think that with more forest, it would work fine, and so....

My rating: ★★★☆☆
